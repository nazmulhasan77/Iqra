# Project Proposal

## Project Title

# Iqra  
### AI-Based Daily Quran Recitation Verification and Progress Tracking Application

---

## 1. Introduction

Many Muslims sincerely wish to recite the Holy Quran every day. However, because of busy schedules, lack of consistency, and limited motivation, maintaining a regular recitation habit can be difficult.

**Iqra** is a proposed smart mobile application designed to help users develop and maintain a daily Quran recitation habit. The application will provide a structured recitation program, daily assigned verses, AI-based voice verification, progress tracking, streaks, reward points, reminders, and a leaderboard.

To participate in a recitation program, a user will be required to pay a subscription fee and receive approval from the system administrator. After joining, the user will receive a specific number of Quran verses each day and will recite them through the application. Artificial Intelligence will analyze the user's voice and verify whether the assigned verses were actually recited.

The system will not mark a daily task as complete simply because the user keeps the screen open or plays an unrelated audio recording. The task will only be completed when the AI verification system confirms that the assigned verses were recited with an acceptable level of accuracy.

---

## 2. Project Summary

The Iqra application will provide each approved and subscribed user with **10 assigned Quran verses every day**. The user will recite the verses using the application's audio recording feature.

The recorded recitation will be processed by an AI-based speech recognition and recitation verification system. The system will compare the detected recitation with the assigned Quranic text and determine whether:

- The correct verses were recited.
- The recitation was sufficiently complete.
- Any verse or important section was skipped.
- The recorded voice was understandable.
- The submission matched the user's assigned daily task.

When verification is successful, the daily task will be marked as **Completed**. The user will receive reward points, and their progress, streak, and leaderboard position will be updated.

If verification fails, the application will notify the user and allow another recitation attempt. Where technically possible, the application may also indicate which verse or section could not be verified.

A subscription fee will be required to join a recitation program. Subscription plans, prices, validity periods, payment methods, renewal rules, and promotional offers will be controlled by the administrator.

---

## 3. Problem Statement

Many existing Quran applications provide features such as Quran reading, translation, audio playback, bookmarks, and tafsir. However, most applications do not verify whether a user has actually completed a daily recitation target.

The major problems addressed by this project are:

1. Users often start regular Quran recitation but fail to maintain consistency.
2. There is usually no reliable method to verify whether assigned verses were actually recited.
3. Users may lack a structured daily recitation plan.
4. Existing applications often provide limited progress monitoring and accountability.
5. There is little integration between recitation verification, streaks, rewards, subscription-based programs, and leaderboards.
6. Administrators of Quran recitation programs may not have an efficient digital system for managing participants, payments, progress, and performance.

Iqra will address these problems through a structured, technology-supported, and AI-verified recitation platform.

## 15. AI Verification Approach

The AI verification module is one of the most important and technically challenging parts of the project.

A possible verification pipeline is:

```text
Recorded Audio
      |
      v
Audio Format Validation
      |
      v
Noise Reduction
      |
      v
Voice Activity Detection
      |
      v
Arabic Speech Recognition
      |
      v
Recognized Text or Phoneme Sequence
      |
      v
Comparison with Assigned Quranic Verses
      |
      v
Sequence and Completeness Analysis
      |
      v
Confidence Score
      |
      v
Pass, Fail, or Manual Review
```

The AI system should consider:

- Different accents and voice characteristics.
- Variations in recitation speed.
- Pauses between verses.
- Background noise.
- Repetition of words or verses.
- Partial recitation.
- Incorrect sequence.
- Low-quality microphones.
- Recorded or replayed audio fraud.

The initial prototype may use a simpler speech-to-text and text-matching approach. A more advanced version may use phoneme-level alignment and Quran-specific Arabic speech models.

## 16. Fraud Prevention

Because the platform includes subscription access, points, rankings, and possible rewards, fraud prevention is necessary.

Possible controls include:

- Detecting repeated use of the same audio.
- Audio fingerprint comparison.
- Limiting daily submission attempts.
- Detecting impossible completion patterns.
- Flagging unusually short recordings.
- Verifying assignment-specific verse sequences.
- Preventing one audio file from being reused by multiple accounts.
- Manual review of suspicious submissions.
- Device and session monitoring, subject to privacy rules.
- Suspending accounts involved in manipulation.

---

## 17. Privacy and Data Protection

The user's voice recording is sensitive personal data. The system must clearly explain:

- Why audio is collected.
- How audio is processed.
- How long audio is stored.
- Whether audio is used to improve the AI model.
- Whether third-party AI services process the audio.
- How users may request deletion.

Recommended privacy practices include:

- Obtain clear user consent before recording.
- Store only necessary information.
- Encrypt audio during transmission.
- Apply restricted access to stored recordings.
- Delete recordings after verification where possible.
- Anonymize data used for model improvement.
- Do not sell user data.
- Provide account and data deletion options.
- Publish a clear privacy policy and terms of service.

## 18. Religious and Ethical Considerations

The application must respect the dignity and sacred nature of the Holy Quran.

Important principles include:

- AI verification must not be presented as a replacement for a qualified Quran teacher.
- The system should avoid giving authoritative religious rulings.
- Tajweed correction should be introduced only with expert guidance and careful validation.
- Quranic text must be obtained from a reliable and verified source.

- Leaderboards should encourage positive participation without promoting pride, humiliation, or unhealthy competition.
- Users should be able to hide their identity or ranking.
- Advertising, payment, and reward policies should remain respectful and transparent.

## 25. Conclusion

**Iqra** is a proposed AI-based Quran recitation and progress-tracking application that will help users build a consistent daily Quran recitation habit.

Users will join a recitation program by paying a subscription fee and receiving admin approval. The application will assign a specific number of verses each day, with a default target of 10 verses. Users will record their recitation through the app, and an AI-based verification system will determine whether the assigned verses were correctly and sufficiently recited.

Successful verification will complete the daily task and update the user's streak, progress, reward points, and leaderboard position. The subscription system will support the operation and development of the platform, while structured programs, reminders, and motivational features will improve user engagement.

With proper technical implementation, religious guidance, privacy protection, transparent payment policies, and responsible AI usage, Iqra can become a reliable and meaningful Islamic technology platform for daily Quran recitation, learning, and long-term habit development.
